A Little About Me

Born in Texas and raised in California with a dash of Taiwan sprinkled in between. I'm a long time supporter, visitor, and lover of museums and other arts spaces. A bookworm at heart with an eye pointed toward projects that let me work and play with the traditional, the new, the innovative, the familiar, and everything in between.

I'm a graduated from Duke University's Master's in Computational Media/Digital Art History program in December 2019. And I'm always looking for more opportunities to be involved with Digital Humanities projects and pedagogy. If you're curious about some of the work I've done thus far you can find my CV here.

Currently: the 2020 Digital Humanities Associate Fellow, Levine Institute for Holocaust Education and Jack, Joseph and Morton Mandel Center for Advanced Holocaust Studies at the United States Holocaust Memorial Museum. Remotely working from my childhood bedroom.
